FAQ

Why work with us?

How can Petainer kegs increase my revenue?

Petainer kegs create opportunities to explore new markets and increase exports. They are pre-purged with N2 and ready to be filled (or can be supplied in preform kits for local manufacture). They eliminate maintenance and return processes, and can generate up to 40% savings on logistics (versus steel kegs).

Why Petainer Kegs?

Petainer kegs help you access low rotation or new export markets and manage seasonal peaks. They increase your flexibility, reduce costs (no Capex), guarantee superior product protection and are environmentally friendly.

Is this information useful?

Why one-way Petainer kegs are more efficient than steel kegs?

Petainer kegs are more efficient than low rotating steel kegs:
- they weigh 10% the weight of steel kegs, reduce transport distance (no returns) and cut transportation costs
- they eliminate costs generated by high steel kegs losses due to theft or damage.
- No Capex and a reduced price pack per L, versus high Capex investments in steel kegs renewals
- No Capex needed to cover seasonal peaks and volume growth, versus high Capex investments required by steel kegs

Will the PET taint/affect the flavor of my product?

No, Petainer has contracted independent taste studies with trained taste panels that confirm that Petainer kegs do not affect the flavour of the beer or wine even after 12 months of storage. The studies also highlight that the PET taste results equal the results of stainless steel kegs.

How to use a keg?

Can a bar's beer line be cleaned while dispensing from a keg?

All Petainer kegs with NPR (no pressure relief) function can get dispensed like steel kegs and for Petainer kegs with PR (pressure relief) function please follow our “use and disposal” instructions related to the type of keg you are using.

Is this information useful?

Are Petainer kegs safe?

Yes, when using Petainer kegs according to our instructions and specifications, Petainer kegs are easy to handle and safe for products. Our kegs are available in two versions, with and without “pressure relief function”. For kegs with pressure relief function, the system is automatically depressurized at decoupling after dispensing. The Classic kegs are protected by outer packaging, either cardboard or a polypropylene box. The Hybrid kegs are puncture resistant and both Classic and Hybrid kegs are supplied with PRTs as standard.

Is this information useful?

What is the shelf life of my product in a Petainer keg?

The shelf life offered by any container is influenced by the sterility of the product inside, the filling process and the sterility of the container. The ability of the container to prevent the ingress of oxygen/egress of CO2 and protect from UV light are also important. Our kegs are delivered clean and microbiologically sterile according to our specifications. Petainer kegs contain an active O2 barrier (oxygen scavenger) and a passive CO2 barrier (Nylon) and have inbuilt light and UV protection up to 500nm.

As such, they can offer a shelf life equal to or better than any “standard” metal containers and provide a filled shelf life of 9 months. Once the keg is tapped and operational, the product inside the keg is recommended to be used with a shelf life of 6-9 weeks (depending on the dispensing hygiene).

Can Petainer kegs be used in hot areas?

To avoid foaming, Petainer kegs should be dispensed up to 30°C /86 F degrees and stored in a cool environment.

What are the risks of keg bursting?

When filled, handled and transported correctly, Petainer kegs are completely safe. For your security, the following should be observed:
Avoid sharp objects.
Maintain the correct working pressure (Max 4.1 bar for Hybrid kegs and 3 bars for Classic).
Always handle with care.
Burst pressure of a petainerKeg is >7bar.

Is a blown keg delivered sterile and pressurized with CO2?

Classic and Hybrid Kegs are delivered clean and microbiologically sterile according to our specifications. They are pressurized with sterile air. Before filling, kegs must be purged (to remove O2) using inert gas - typically CO2 or N2. Hybrid kegs are delivered pre-flushed with N2 (>99% N2 in the keg). Both Classic and Hybrid kegs produced in the US are pre-flushed with N2 (>99% N2 in the keg, 0,7 bar/10,5PSI)

What happens if a keg gets hot inside a cargo container?

Like any pressurized containers, Petainer kegs are completely safe when transported within the correct temperature guidelines. They should be stored at 20 – 22°C/68-72 F, although higher temperatures (up to 30°C/86 F) can be tolerated for short periods of up to one week. Temperature tests of up to 50°C/122 F have been undertaken. Results indicate that Petainer kegs still perform within specifications, but the shelf life of the product is reduced.
